## Handling Objections

### "This looks great, but I need to think about it."
**Response:** "Of course. Here's what I propose—let me build this for one client, completely free. You can measure the actual time savings and see how your client responds. If it doesn't deliver value, you haven't lost anything. But if it does, we roll it out to everyone. Fair enough?"

### "Can you integrate with our actual data?"
**Response:** "Absolutely. This connects to Google Analytics 4, Google Search Console, and Google Business Profile. We can also integrate call tracking, CRM systems, and custom data sources. The foundation is built for real-world data."

### "Will my clients actually use this?"
**Response:** "We designed it specifically for busy business owners. It's mobile-friendly, uses plain English, and takes 30 seconds to understand. Most clients check it weekly without any prompting. And when they do, they see your value instantly."

### "What about security?"
**Response:** "Enterprise-grade security. Encrypted connections, role-based access, and client data isolation. Your clients only see their data—never another client's information. We use the same security standards as banks."

### "How long to build the real version?"
**Response:** "2-3 weeks for the first client, 4-6 weeks for full deployment. The foundation is already built. Most of the work is connecting your actual data sources and customizing for your specific needs."

## Key Phrases to Use

- "You told me..." (shows you listened)
- "Imagine..." (helps him visualize)
- "This means..." (connects features to benefits)
- "Instead of..." (contrasts old vs new way)
- "What if..." (opens possibilities)

---

## Demo Don'ts

❌ Don't talk too fast—let Jeff absorb  
❌ Don't show every feature—focus on what matters to him  
❌ Don't get too technical—keep it business-focused  
❌ Don't rush to close—let him come to the conclusion  
❌ Don't oversell—let the demo speak for itself  

---

## Demo Do's

✅ Do ask questions—"What do you think?"  
✅ Do relate to his business—"For your electrical clients..."  
✅ Do show enthusiasm—this is exciting!  
✅ Do pause for reactions—watch his face  
✅ Do end with a clear next step
