## 6. SALES CONVERSATION GUIDE

### 6.1 Discovery Questions

#### Understanding Current State

1. **"Walk me through your current reporting process. How much time are you spending on reports each week?"**
   - Listen for: Time investment, pain points, frustration level
   - Follow-up: "How does that time impact your ability to focus on strategy and sales?"

2. **"When you send reports to clients, what kind of questions do they typically ask?"**
   - Listen for: Understanding gaps, communication challenges, trust issues
   - Follow-up: "How often do you have to schedule calls just to explain the reports?"

3. **"How do you currently show clients that their investment is paying off?"**
   - Listen for: Proof methods, client satisfaction, retention challenges
   - Follow-up: "Have you ever lost a client who said they 'weren't seeing results'?"

4. **"Tell me about your team's decision-making process. How often do they need your input on client campaigns?"**
   - Listen for: Team independence, bottlenecks, scalability concerns
   - Follow-up: "What would change if your team could make confident decisions without checking with you?"

#### Identifying Pain Points

5. **"What's your biggest frustration when it comes to client communication?"**
   - Listen for: Primary pain point, emotional trigger
   - Probe deeper based on response

6. **"If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about your client relationships, what would it be?"**
   - Listen for: Aspirations, desired outcomes, priorities
   - This reveals what they value most

7. **"How do you currently differentiate yourself from other SEO agencies when pitching new clients?"**
   - Listen for: Competitive challenges, unique positioning, sales obstacles
   - Follow-up: "What would give you an edge in those competitive situations?"

8. **"What's your current client churn rate? Do you know why clients leave?"**
   - Listen for: Retention metrics, reasons for churn, financial impact
   - Follow-up: "What would a 10-20% improvement in retention mean for your business?"

#### Understanding Goals

9. **"Where do you want your agency to be in 12 months? What's holding you back from getting there?"**
   - Listen for: Growth goals, obstacles, priorities
   - Connect dashboard to their specific goals

10. **"What would success look like for you with a dashboard solution?"**
    - Listen for: Expectations, definition of value, success metrics
    - Use this to tailor the proposal

11. **"If you had 10 extra hours a week, what would you focus on?"**
    - Listen for: Priorities, high-value activities, opportunity cost
    - Quantify the value of time savings

12. **"How important is it for your clients to feel like partners rather than just customers?"**
    - Listen for: Relationship philosophy, client experience priorities
    - Connect to feedback loop and collaboration features

### 6.2 Objection Handling

#### Objection: "I don't have budget for this right now."

**Response:**
> "I completely understand. Let me ask you this—how much is your current reporting process costing you? If you're spending 10+ hours a week at $150/hour, that's $6,000 a month in time costs alone. 
>
> Here's what I propose: Let's start with a free proof-of-concept for one client. You can measure the actual time savings and see how your client responds. If it doesn't save you at least 5 hours in the first month, you haven't lost anything. But if it does, we've just found budget you didn't know you had. Fair enough?"

#### Objection: "My clients are happy with the current reports."

**Response:**
> "That's great—it means you're already delivering value. But let me ask: When you send those reports, how many clients actually read them thoroughly versus asking you to explain over the phone?
>
> The agencies we work with often find that clients are 'satisfied' but not 'engaged.' A dashboard doesn't just report—it creates a collaboration experience. Clients can leave feedback, ask questions, and feel like true partners. 
>
> Would you be open to testing it with one client and getting their honest feedback? Sometimes we don't know what we're missing until we see something better."

#### Objection: "I can build this myself with Google Data Studio."

**Response:**
> "You're absolutely right—you could. And if you have 40+ hours to learn the platform, set up the integrations, design the layouts, and maintain it ongoing, that might be the right path.
>
> But let me ask: Is that the best use of your time? At $150/hour, those 40 hours represent $6,000 in opportunity cost. Plus ongoing maintenance and updates.
>
> Our clients tell us the real value isn't just the dashboard—it's the strategic thinking that goes into what metrics matter, how to present them, and how to use the data to drive decisions. That's what we bring.
>
> What if we built you a proof-of-concept in a week, and you can decide if the time savings and expertise are worth the investment?"

#### Objection: "I need to think about it."

**Response:**
> "Of course, this is an important decision. Can I ask what specific concerns you'd like to think through? I want to make sure you have all the information you need.
>
> [Listen and address specific concerns]
>
> Here's what I'd suggest: Let's schedule a 20-minute demo where I can show you exactly how this would work with your data. No commitment, just a chance to see it in action. If after that you still want to think about it, that's completely fine. But at least you'll be making an informed decision. How does Tuesday or Wednesday work for you?"

#### Objection: "My team won't use it."

**Response:**
> "That's a valid concern—adoption is critical. Here's what we've learned: Teams resist tools that add work or complexity. But they embrace tools that make their jobs easier.
>
> Our dashboard is designed to reduce work, not add it. No more manual report creation. No more digging through multiple platforms for data. No more wondering if a campaign is working.
>
> Plus, we include comprehensive training and ongoing support. Most teams are up and running confidently within a week.
>
> What if we included team training as part of the proof-of-concept? You can see firsthand how your team responds before making any commitment."

#### Objection: "I'm not sure my clients would use it."

**Response:**
> "That's exactly why we start with a proof-of-concept. Let's pick one client—maybe one who's been asking more questions lately or one you want to strengthen the relationship with.
>
> We'll build them a custom dashboard and you can see their reaction firsthand. Most clients are thrilled to have transparent access to their data. It builds trust and reduces those 'what's happening with my campaign?' emails.
>
> If your client doesn't love it, you've lost nothing. But if they do, you have a powerful new tool for retention and referrals. Sound like a low-risk way to test it?"
